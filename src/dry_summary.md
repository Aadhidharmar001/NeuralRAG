# Reinforcement Schedules
- **Continuous Reinforcement** – Reinforce every occurrence; leads to fast learning but quick extinction when reinforcement stops.  
- **Partial Reinforcement** – Reinforce only some occurrences; acquisition is slower but the behavior shows higher resistance to extinction.  
- **Partial Schedule Types** – Fixed Ratio, Variable Ratio, Fixed Interval, Variable Interval.

# Generalization & Discrimination
- **Generalization** – The behavior appears in contexts that are similar to the original reinforcement context.  
- **Discrimination** – The ability to distinguish between stimuli or situations and respond only to those that have been reinforced.

# Techniques for Improving Study Skills
1. **Mnemonic Devices (Acronyms)** – e.g., *VIBGYOR* for rainbow colors.  
2. **Method of Loci** – Visualize items placed in familiar locations (mental “walk”).  
3. **Keyword System** – Link foreign words to familiar English words using imagery (e.g., *Pato* → pot + duck).  
4. **Pegword System** – Memorize a set of peg words and attach new items to these pegs.  
5. **Chunking** – Group information into meaningful units (e.g., credit‑card number → historical dates).  
6. **Link Method (Narrative Method)** – Connect unrelated items into a story or chain of images.

# STUDY HABITS
*(Opening anecdote: a whimsical story about a person in a bird costume, a mailbox on his head, jumping into a river, illustrating that unrelated items can be linked in a narrative.)*

1. **Knowledge of Result (Feedback)** – Use self‑recitation to gauge progress.  
2. **Recitation** – Summarize aloud after reading; restate in own words to reinforce memory.  
3. **Rehearsal** – Mental review of material as you read; the more rehearsal, the better retention.  
4. **Selection** – Actively highlight only the most important points; avoid over‑marking everything.  
5. **Organization** – Chunk information into meaningful groups (e.g., directional words, colors, people).  
6. **Whole vs. Part Learning** – Prefer whole‑unit learning for short, organized material; use part‑learning for highly complex content.  
7. **Serial Position Effect** – Extra practice on middle items of a list to counter the tendency to forget them.  
8. **Overlearning** – Continue studying beyond mastery to guard against test anxiety and blank‑out moments.  
9. **Spaced Practice** – Alternate short study sessions with brief rest periods; more effective than marathon sessions.  
10. **Sleep** – Sleep after studying.
